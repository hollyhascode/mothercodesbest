Tire Chain Finder

This was a fun project where we wanted to setup an aesthetically attractive and easy-to-use tire chain finder. Unfortunately, the tire chains ended up being too pricey to make sense to sell at the company I work for, but we didn't know this before I built out the project.

I converted an excel file to JSON then used XML and jQuery UI autocomplete to create the search. In addition to functionality, I did the page layout (not the photographs though).

Here is a screenshot of the general layout. The client prefers longer landing pages, but I wanted the main focus to be just entering a tire size.
The contact form at the bottom has a parralex background of a snow scene - this came out silly looking in the screenshot.
The aesthetics of this project were fun! Who doesn't like snow - or at least pictures of it! My favorite part though was learning more about ajax, json, jquery and react throughout the process.
From a business point of view, I learned the pain of creating something beautiful and functional that will probably never be used - the company decided in the end to not sell tire chains.
From a designer's point of view, I learned to not be so forward with animations. The categories have 3d flip cards that transform with CSS - this is buggy in internet explorer. I also find it personally to be to flashy. The owner and vice-president of the company LOVE it though, so now they want it on all of the front pages of their websites.
To remedy this problem, I plan to do some UX experiments after launch to test the usability of the flip boxes. Given our demographic is mainly older blue collar males looking for a simple product, my hypothesis is that it won't do so well.
